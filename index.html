I am a Ph.D. student at the School of Computer Science, Peking University. I received my B.Sc. in Data Science and Big Data Technology from the School of Computer Science, Central South University. My research centers on large language models (LLMs) and their application to search & recommendation systems.

More specifically, I am interested in LLM-based listwise ranking and re-ranking, search-quality evaluation, and building reliable LLM agents that can reason and use tools effectively. I am also drawn to reinforcement learning as a way to better align these systems with real user satisfaction.

Alongside my academic work, I have interned at Alibaba’s Qwen Business Unit and Kuaishou’s Search Technology Department, where I developed LLM-driven retrieval and search algorithms for large-scale production systems. These experiences reinforce my belief that good research should ultimately translate into a better real-world user experience.

My recent research includes LENS, a skill-based framework for listwise search-quality evaluation (co-first author, under review at CIKM 2026), and SPDTM, a Shapley-value-based method for mobile crowd sensing (Computer Networks, 2025). I have also received the National Scholarship (2024 and 2025) and been named an Outstanding Student.

I was born in June 2004 and come from Longyan, Fujian, China. Feel free to reach out to me via email if you would like to discuss research or collaboration.

selected publications

  1. CIKM
    LENS: Leveraging Explainable Skills for Listwise Evaluation in Kuaishou Search
    Zhongxin Huang* and others
    Proceedings of the 32nd ACM International Conference on Information and Knowledge Management (CIKM), 2026
    Under review
    Abs Bib

    A skill-based whole-page search quality evaluation framework enabling automated GSB evaluation and bad-case attribution. We propose a two-stage SFT + DAPO-based training scheme whose offline classification accuracy and attribution recall clearly surpass mainstream LLM baselines. Online human spot-check consistency reached 84.2%, with evaluation efficiency 40.6x over manual annotation on L20 machines.

  2. CompNet
    SPDTM: A Shapley-value Based Preference Discovery and Task Matching to Maximize Satisfaction in Mobile Crowd Sensing
    Zhongxin Huang, Zhijun Sun, Wenbo Tan, and 4 more authors
    Computer Networks, Oct 2025
    Abs DOI Bib

    A Shapley-value based reinforcement learning framework for preference discovery and task matching in Mobile Crowd Sensing (MCS), addressing low task-worker matching efficiency and false preference reporting. Uses a three-stage design with MADDPG and multi-armed bandit; on real datasets, user preference recognition reached 94.79% and task completion rate 91.77%, outperforming baselines.
